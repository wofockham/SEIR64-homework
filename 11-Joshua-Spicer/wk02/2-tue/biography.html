Biography

John von Neumann was a Hungarian-American mathematician, physicist, computer scientist, and polymath who made significant contributions to numerous fields during his lifetime.

He is perhaps best known for his work on the development of the modern computer, including his invention of the Von Neumann architecture, which is still used in computer design today.

Von Neumann also made contributions to the fields of game theory, quantum mechanics, and economics, among others. He was a prolific author and lecturer, and his work continues to be studied and applied in many fields today.
