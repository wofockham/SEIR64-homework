John von Neumann

Biography

John von Neumann was a Hungarian-American mathematician, physicist, and computer scientist who made significant contributions to a wide range of fields, including mathematics, physics, economics, game theory, and computer science.

He was born on December 28, 1903, in Budapest, Hungary, and showed an early aptitude for mathematics. He earned a Ph.D. in mathematics from the University of Budapest at the age of 22.

In 1930, he emigrated to the United States, where he began working at Princeton University. During World War II, he worked on the Manhattan Project, which developed the first atomic bomb.

Von Neumann made important contributions to the development of game theory, the mathematical foundations of quantum mechanics, and the concept of the stored-program computer.
