About Norbert Wiener

Norbert Wiener (November 26, 1894 to March 18, 1964) was an American mathematician and philosopher who contributed significantly to the development of cybernetics, a field of study focused on the control and communication of information in complex systems.

Education

Wiener earned his PhD in mathematics from Harvard University in 1913, at the age of 18. He also studied at Cambridge University and the University of GÃ¶ttingen in Germany.

Career

After completing his PhD, Wiener worked at a number of institutions, including Harvard University, MIT, and the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. He made significant contributions to a number of fields, including stochastic processes, Fourier analysis, and information theory.
