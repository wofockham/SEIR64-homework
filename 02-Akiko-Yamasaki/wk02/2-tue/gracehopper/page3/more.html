Language

Hopper joined develping the UNIVAC, which was the large-scale electronic computer to be on the markeint in the 50s. It was at these time that she recommended the development of a new programming language that would use entirely English words. She "was told very quickly that [she] couldn't do this because computers didn't understand English. Despite all the scepticism, she succededly invented a compiler. operational link-loader, which at the time was referred to as a compiler. She later said that "Nobody believed that," and that she "had a running compiler and nobody would touch it. They told me computers could only do arithmetic.
