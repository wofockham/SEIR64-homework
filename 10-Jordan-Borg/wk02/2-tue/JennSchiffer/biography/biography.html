Biography of Jenn Schiffer

Did you know this about Jenn??!?

  • Jenn Schiffer is an American artist, programmer, writer and educator, best known for her satirical, irreverent and often hilarious web art and coding projects. Born in New Jersey in 1984, Schiffer grew up in a creative family and was encouraged to explore her interests in art and technology from a young age.
  • After earning a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in Graphic Design from Ramapo College of New Jersey in 2006, Schiffer began working as a front-end web developer, honing her skills in HTML, CSS and JavaScript. At the same time, she continued to explore her passion for art and illustration, creating digital works that combined her technical expertise with her keen sense of humor and love of pop culture.
  • In 2013, Schiffer co-founded the internet collective and studio 8 Bit Artifacts, which specializes in creating web art and tools that push the boundaries of traditional design and programming. She has since gone on to gain a cult following for her viral web projects, such as "Make 8-bit art!", a free online tool for creating retro-style pixel art, and "Myspace Tom's reactions to Facebook reactions", a hilarious commentary on the changing nature of social media.
  • In addition to her artistic pursuits, Schiffer is also a dedicated educator and mentor, teaching coding and design to students of all ages through her work with organizations like Girls Who Code and Code Liberation. She is also a frequent speaker and commentator on issues related to technology, art and culture, and has been featured in publications such as Wired, Fast Company and The New York Times.
  • Overall, Jenn Schiffer is a highly talented and original artist and programmer, who has made a name for herself through her innovative and irreverent web art and coding projects. She continues to inspire and engage audiences around the world through her work, and is widely recognized as one of the leading voices in the intersection of art and technology today.
