HONOURS & AWARDS

Some of her many accomplishments include:

  • Elected as a Fellow of the British Royal Society.
  • Elected as a Fellow of the British Royal Academy of Engineering.
  • Awarded the Fellow Award by the Computer History Museum in California in 2012 for her work, with Steve Furber, on the BBC Micro computer and the ARM processor architecture.
  • Received the 2014 Lovie Lifetime Achievement Award in acknowledgement for her invention of the ARM processor.
  • Became an honorary fellow of her alma mater, Selwyn College, Cambridge.
  • Listed in Maximum PC as number 8 in an article titled "The 15 Most Important Women in Tech History".
  • Appointed Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE) in the 2019 Birthday Honours for services to computing.
  • Honoured as a Distinguished Fellow of the British Computer Society.
  • Played a cameo role as a pub landlady in the BBC television drama Micro Men, based on the beginnings of the ARM microprocessor.
