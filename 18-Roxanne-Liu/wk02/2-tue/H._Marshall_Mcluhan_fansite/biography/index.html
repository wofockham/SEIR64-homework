Biography

H. Marshall McLuhan, 1974.

H. Marshall McLuhan was a Canadian philosopher, professor, and public intellectual. He was born on July 21, 1911 in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada.

McLuhan received his B.A. from the University of Manitoba in 1933 and his M.A. and Ph.D. in English literature from the University of Cambridge in 1934 and 1943, respectively. He taught at several universities throughout his career, including the University of Toronto, where he served as director of the Centre for Culture and Technology from 1963 to 1979.He was also a popular lecturer.

McLuhan is best known for his work on media theory, particularly his ideas about the effects of mass media on society and culture. He coined the phrase "the medium is the message" to express his belief that the way in which information is conveyed is more important than the content of the message itself.

McLuhan passed away on December 31, 1980 in Toronto, Canada.
