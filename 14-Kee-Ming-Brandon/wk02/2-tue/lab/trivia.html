Little known detail about John von Neumann

Von Neumann was a child prodigy who was able to divide two eight-digit numbers in his head by the age of six.

He spoke several languages fluently, including Hungarian, German, English, French, and Italian.

He was a key figure in the development of the Monte Carlo method, a statistical technique used to simulate complex systems.

Von Neumann was also a consultant for the US government on a range of issues, including nuclear weapons, missile defense, and computer development.

Von Neumann was an avid gambler and used his expertise in probability and game theory to develop winning strategies in games of chance.

Von Neumann was known for his love of practical jokes, and he once rigged a colleague's office with hidden microphones and speakers so he could control the volume of the man's voice.

Despite being a brilliant mathematician, von Neumann struggled with spelling, and his colleagues often had to correct his spelling mistakes in his papers and correspondence.

He was involved in the Manhattan Project, which developed the first atomic bomb during World War II, and he helped to design the bomb's explosive mechanism.
