Biography

Yukihiro Matsumoto, also known as "Matz", is
a computer scientist and software programmer from Japan. He was born on April 14, 1965, in Osaka, Japan.

He is best known for creating the Ruby programming language in 1993, which he released to the public in 1995. Matsumoto was an expert on multiple programming languages at the time, including Perl, Eiffel, Smalltalk, Lisp, and Ada. When creating Ruby, he tried to combine what he thought were the best elements of each of these languages.

Matsumoto is also an avid musician and plays several instruments, including the guitar and bass. He has released several albums of music and has performed in concerts with other musicians.

He has received numerous awards for his contributions to the field of computer science, including the 2011 ACM Software System Award and the 2014 IEEE John von Neumann Medal.
