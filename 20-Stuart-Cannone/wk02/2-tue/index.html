Alan Turing

The Man That Broke The Enigma

Alan Turing (1912-1954) was a brilliant mathematician, logician, and computer scientist who is widely considered one of the most important figures in the history of computing. He is best known for his work during World War II as a codebreaker at Bletchley Park, where he helped decipher German communications, including the notorious Enigma code.

Turing's contributions to the development of early computers and artificial intelligence were also groundbreaking. He proposed the concept of a universal machine that could perform any computational task, which became the basis for modern computers. Additionally, he developed the "Turing Test," a benchmark for measuring a machine's ability to exhibit human-like intelligence.
