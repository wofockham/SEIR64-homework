Concepts

Turing Machine

The Turing Machine is a theoretical model of a simple computing device that was proposed by Alan Turing in 1936. It is a thought experiment that explores the nature and limits of computation. The Turing machine is a theoretical construct that is used to understand the principles of computation and to investigate the concept of an algorithm.

Turing Test

The Turing Test is a method proposed by Alan Turing in 1950 to test a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior that is indistinguishable from that of a human. The Turing Test involves a human evaluator engaging in a natural language conversation with a machine and a human, without knowing which is which. The machine is said to have passed the test if the evaluator cannot reliably distinguish between the machine and the human based on their responses.

Turing completeness

Alan Turing proposed the concept of Turing completeness, which is a measure of a system's ability to perform any computation that can be performed by a Turing machine. A system that is Turing complete is said to be capable of performing any computation that can be performed by any other computer.
